Discover Amsterdam

Amsterdam, the capital of the Netherlands, is known for its artistic heritage, elaborate canal system, and narrow houses with gabled facades. The city is rich with cultural experiences—from world-renowned museums like the Van Gogh Museum and Rijksmuseum to its buzzing bike culture and scenic tulip-filled parks. With a welcoming vibe and a vibrant atmosphere, Amsterdam offers a perfect mix of history, innovation, and leisure.

Top Destinations

Canal Belt

Enjoy a serene boat ride along Amsterdam’s iconic 17th-century canals.

Rijksmuseum

Explore Dutch masterpieces from artists like Rembrandt and Vermeer.

Van Gogh Museum

Home to the world’s largest collection of works by Vincent van Gogh.

Anne Frank House

Visit the moving museum where Anne Frank wrote her famous diary.

Dam Square

The historical heart of Amsterdam with the Royal Palace and lively atmosphere.

Jordaan

Charming neighborhood known for its narrow streets, art galleries, and cafés.

Festivals of Amsterdam

King’s Day

A national celebration with parades, markets, and orange-themed festivities.

Light Festival

A winter wonderland of light installations illuminating the canals.

Tulip Festival

Celebrate spring with vibrant tulip displays across the city.

Adventure Activities

Bike Tours

Cycle like a local and explore the hidden gems of Amsterdam.

Canal Boating

Rent a boat or join a cruise to see the city from the water.

Watersports

Try your hand at water sports like wakeboarding, surfing, or jet surfing.

Special Foods

Stroopwafel

Sweet caramel waffles best enjoyed warm from local markets.

Herring

Try the Dutch delicacy of raw herring served with onions and pickles.

Dutch Pancakes

Thin, large pancakes with sweet or savory toppings.

Patat

Thick-cut fries served with a variety of sauces.

Appeltaart

Dutch apple pie, a classic sweet treat often served with cream.

Bitterballen

Crispy meat-filled snacks perfect with a cold drink.

Hotels & Stays

Amstel Hotel

Luxury stay by the river with classic architecture and fine dining.

Conservatorium

Modern design meets comfort in Amsterdam’s museum district.

Houseboats

Unique accommodation experience floating on Amsterdam's canals.

Must Buys

Delftware Pottery

Blue-and-white painted ceramics, including vases, plates, and tiles.

Wooden Clogs

Traditional Dutch footwear, often ornately painted and symbolic of Dutch heritage.

Dutch Cheese

Widely known for varieties like Gouda and Edam, often sold in colorful wheels.

Transportation Guide

Getting around Amsterdam is simple and fun:

    Airport: Amsterdam Schiphol Airport (AMS)

    Public Transport: Trams, buses, metros, and ferries via GVB network

    Bike: Rent a bike to travel like a local across the flat, scenic city
