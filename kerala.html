Discover Kerala

Kerala, known as "God’s Own Country," is a paradise of lush greenery, pristine backwaters, and golden beaches. A land where nature, culture, and tradition come alive in perfect harmony. From the misty hills of Munnar to the serene backwaters of Alleppey, Kerala offers breathtaking landscapes, vibrant festivals, and mouthwatering cuisine. Explore the rich heritage of Kochi, unwind on the pristine beaches of Varkala, or experience the tranquility of Ayurveda. Whether it's the spice plantations, wildlife sanctuaries, or houseboat cruises, Kerala promises an unforgettable journey through God’s Own Country.

Top Destinations

Munnar

Rolling hills and tea plantations make Munnar a dream destination for nature lovers.

Alleppey

Famous for its mesmerizing backwaters and luxurious houseboat cruises.

Kovalam

Home to stunning beaches and vibrant seaside resorts.

Kochi

A vibrant port city blending colonial charm, backwaters, and modern culture.

Wayanad

A nature lover's paradise with lush forests, waterfalls, and wildlife.

Trivandrum

Kerala’s capital city, where heritage, beaches, and modernity blend seamlessly.

Festivals of Kerala

Onam

A 10-day harvest festival celebrating Kerala’s culture with boat races, floral carpets, and grand feasts.

Thrissur Pooram

The most vibrant temple festival, famous for its grand elephant processions and fireworks.

Thrikarthika

A radiant festival of lights in Kerala, celebrating devotion with lamps and traditional rituals.

Adventure Activities

Houseboat Cruise

Stay in a floating houseboat and enjoy the serene backwaters of Kerala.

Trekking

Explore breathtaking trails in Wayanad and Munnar.

Snorkeling

Experience the vibrant marine life in the waters of Kovalam.

Special Foods

Palada Payasam

A rich and creamy dessert made with rice flakes, milk, and sugar, a must-have in Kerala feasts.

Malabar Parota

A flaky, layered flatbread from Kerala’s Malabar region, best paired with spicy curries.

Karimeen Pollichathu

A Kerala delicacy of pearl spot fish marinated in spices and wrapped in banana leaves for grilling.

Puttu & Kadala Curry

A classic Kerala breakfast of steamed rice cakes served with spicy black chickpea curry.

Sadhya

A grand vegetarian feast served on a banana leaf, featuring an array of traditional Kerala dishes.

Thalassery Biryani

A fragrant and flavorful Malabar-style biryani, known for its unique blend of spices and Jeerakasala rice.

Resort

Old Harbour Hotel

A charming heritage hotel in Fort Kochi, blending colonial elegance with modern luxury.

Kumarakom Lake Resort

A serene backwater retreat offering traditional Kerala architecture and breathtaking views.

Hotel Raviz Resort

A luxurious escape on the banks of Ashtamudi Lake, showcasing Kerala’s rich heritage and tranquility.

Must Buys

Banana Chips

A popular snacks item of Kerala made of raw banana fried in coconut oil.

Coir Products

Eco-friendly products made from coconut husk, including mats and rugs.

Ayurvedic Products:

Kerala is a major center for Ayurveda, so you can find a range of herbal products, oils, and remedies.

Transportation Guide

Kerala is well connected by air, rail, and road. Here are the best ways to travel around:

    Airports: Kochi, Trivandrum, Calicut International Airports

    Railways: Extensive train network connecting major cities

    Road: State-run and private buses, taxis, and rental vehicles
